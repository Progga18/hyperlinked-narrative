What is Ashtanga Yoga?

Ashtanga Yoga is a rigorous, structured style of yoga rooted in ancient tradition and revitalized in modern times by Sri K. Pattabhi Jois. It combines breath, movement, and gaze (known as tristhana) in a precise sequence of poses. The word "Ashtanga" means "eight limbs," a reference to the eightfold path described in the Yoga Sutras of Patanjali, which includes ethical disciplines, physical postures, breath control, sensory withdrawal, concentration, meditation, and blissful absorption.

A Brief History of Ashtaga Yoga

The modern lineage of Ashtanga Yoga begins with Sri T. Krishnamacharya, considered the grandfather of modern yoga. One of his most notable students was Pattabhi Jois, who developed the Ashtanga method based on a text called the Yoga Korunta. Jois began teaching Ashtanga Yoga in Mysore, India, in the 1940s. The practice spread globally through dedicated students who traveled to Mysore to study under Jois. In the 1990s and 2000s, Ashtanga gained widespread popularity in the West. Today, the legacy continues through the Sharath Yoga Centre (formerly KPJAYI) in Mysore, led by Sharath Jois, Pattabhi Jois's grandson.
